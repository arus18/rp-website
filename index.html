Introducing KiddoCare Transforming Daycare with AI Smart Solutions for Safer Play and Smarter Learning

Discover Safe Play and Smart Growth: Kiddocare, Your AI-Enhanced Daycare Companion, Unleashes Your Child's Potential with Cutting-Edge Technology.

Literature survey

The Challenges and Effects of Busy Lifestyles
Parents' hectic schedules necessitate hard work to secure their family's welfare. Research shows both positive and negative effects on children due to parents' busy lives. Positive outcomes include a better quality of life, valuable life experiences, time management skills, independence, and stress management. However, the negative impacts on children's physical and mental health are more pronounced.
A study involving 2987 elementary school children in Tokyo's Adachi district highlighted the adverse effects of limited parental time on children's mental health, including reduced parent-child interaction and increased anxiety and stress. Similarly, in the Brasov District, Romania, a study linked parents' busy lives to unhealthy dietary habits in children, resulting in obesity and reduced physical activity.

Daycare centers also play a crucial role in cognitive and athletic development. Mobile games have emerged as effective tools for enhancing children's cognitive skills. Enhancing Daycare Safety and Monitoring
Several research initiatives focus on improving daycare safety. For instance, one system uses GPS to monitor parents' arrival for child pick-up, enhancing security. Another system employs RFID technology to ensure children's safety during school transportation. Additionally, facial recognition systems using CCTV help parents monitor their children's activities at daycare centers. In Sri Lanka, a survey of primary school teachers indicated moderate support for integrating mobile games into teaching. Furthermore, the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) concepts in educational games is gaining traction, helping students grasp fundamental concepts effectively.

Research gap

Our research addresses a significant gap in current childcare literature by focusing on the emotional dynamics during child pickup and the transition to parental care. While previous studies have explored various aspects of childcare,
such as child monitoring, communication between parents and teachers, daycare selection, and safety during transportation, they often overlook the emotional well-being of parents and children during pickup. Our research seeks to fill
this gap by examining the emotional aspects of this crucial moment in childcare.
Specifically, we introduce an innovative educational game designed for daycare centers, which utilizes facial recognition technology to personalize learning experiences for each child. Additionally, our system provides daily progress
reports to parents and daycare staff, offering insights into each child's achievements and areas for improvement. Furthermore, in cases where a child's progress declines, our system suggests personalized activities in collaboration with
parents and staff, fostering a supportive learning environment.
Understanding teachers' attitudes towards integrating technology into classrooms is pivotal for the successful implementation of our system. Building on previous research demonstrating the positive impact of educational mobile games on
preschool cognitive development, our approach emphasizes game-based learning inspired by Piaget's theory. Moreover, our research aligns with studies highlighting the effectiveness of digital tools in enhancing teaching methods, guided
by quality standards.

Research problem

In the hustle and bustle of modern life, childcare centers have become crucial for parents balancing multiple responsibilities, as reported by the National Child Protection Authority, with over 17,020 in Sri Lanka alone. However, our examination of these centers has revealed significant issues that demand attention.
One major concern is the lack of insight among parents into their children's characteristics and abilities due to hectic schedules. This gap can lead to a disconnect between parents and children, impacting the child's decision-making process. Additionally, parents often remain uninformed about daycare activities until drop-off and pick-up times.
Child protection is another critical issue, with instances of parents failing to pick up their children on time or delegating the task to others. Mental fatigue or stress during pickup can further endanger the child's well-being, posing risks like vehicular accidents.
Despite some studies exploring children's behavior in daycare settings and employing GPS technology, comprehensive solutions remain elusive.
Therefore, our research introduces a daycare system integrating safety mechanisms, including harmful object and activity detection. This system ensures secure handovers and communicates insights about children's interests and abilities to parents through a dedicated app. Through these interventions, we aim to enhance the daycare experience and promote the well-being of children and families.

Research Objectives

Our aim is to create an innovative childcare system that prioritizes safety, engagement, and cognitive development for children. Here are our objectives:

  1. Develop personalized learning: Identify children's interests based on their play activities to create a personalized and engaging environment for learning and play.

  2. Enhance pick-up process: Improve efficiency and security during child pick-up through facial recognition and emotional analysis, ensuring a smoother and more positive experience for both guardians and children.

  3. Ensure safety: Implement a system to detect and address potential hazards within the daycare environment, ensuring a safer and more secure setting for children to explore and learn.

  4. Stimulate cognitive development: Integrate educational games and activities into the daycare curriculum to improve children's cognitive abilities and strengthen parent-child relationships.

Economy Feasibility: Costs include cloud hosting, high-quality CCTV cameras, OpenAI API key, and webcams. Cloud hosting covers storage, processing power, and bandwidth. CCTV cameras are durable, and webcams offer precise face emotion detection.

Technical Feasibility: Requires machine learning expertise, front-end development skills (React.js, Node.js), back-end development (Python, Flask), NoSQL database management (MongoDB), API integration (OpenAI), and UI/UX design.
